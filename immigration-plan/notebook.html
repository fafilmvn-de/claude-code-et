🛬 Part 1 — Day 0: Port of Entry
Each family member's passport contains an Immigrant Visa foil stamped by the US Embassy in Hanoi. Principal investor: CR5. Spouse and children: CR6. The principal investor holds a sealed NVC/Embassy packet — do NOT open it. Hand it directly to the CBP officer.

Step-by-Step POE Procedure

  1. Primary Inspection — Present passport + sealed embassy packet. CBP scans passport, verifies biometrics.
  2. Secondary Inspection (expected for first-time immigrant entry — normal) — CBP processes sealed packet, formally admits you as a Conditional Permanent Resident. Expect 30 min to 2+ hours.
  3. I-551 ADIT Stamp — Passport stamped with I-551 (valid 6–12 months). Functions as temporary green card for employment, banking, SSN, driver's license. Physical green card arrives by mail 45–90 days later.
  4. After customs — Your family is now officially Conditional Permanent Residents of the United States.

📊 Part 7 — Month 2–12: Ongoing Operations

US Taxes — Year 1 Critical Guidance

Dual-status year (2026): January–May 2026 = Non-Resident Alien. June–December 2026 = Resident Alien. You file a dual-status tax return for 2026. This is complex — hire a US-Vietnam CPA for Year 1. Do not attempt this yourself. Expect $500–$2,000 for Year 1 dual-status return.

From Day 1 of US residency, all worldwide income is reportable: Vietnamese employment income, rental income from Vietnamese property, interest/dividends from Vietnamese bank accounts, capital gains from Vietnamese investments.

FBAR — FinCEN Form 114 (Annual Foreign Bank Reporting)

  • Required if combined maximum balance in all foreign accounts exceeds $10,000 at any point during the calendar year
  • File at bsaefiling.fincen.treas.gov — NOT with your tax return
  • Deadline: April 15 (automatic extension to October 15)
  • Report ALL Vietnamese bank accounts (savings, checking, business)
  • Non-willful penalty: up to ~$15,000 per violation per year
  • Willful failure: up to $100,000 or 50% of account balance + criminal charges

FATCA — Form 8938 (Foreign Financial Assets)

Filed WITH your Form 1040. Married Filing Jointly threshold (living in US): report if total foreign financial assets exceed $100,000 on last day of year OR $150,000 on any day during year.

No US-Vietnam Tax Treaty. Vietnam and the US do not have a tax treaty. You cannot claim treaty exemptions. Claim the Foreign Tax Credit (Form 1116) to avoid double taxation — Vietnamese tax paid can be credited against US tax on the same income.

📄 Part 8 — I-829: Removing Conditions on Permanent Residence
🔴 This is the second most critical legal deadline. The I-829 must be filed within the 90-day period immediately preceding the 2nd anniversary of your date of admission. Missing this deadline means automatic status termination and immediate removability. No grace period.

Calculating Your 90-Day Window

Anniversary date = your exact POE entry date + 2 years

MilestoneExample (Entry: June 15, 2026)
Entry dateJune 15, 2026
2-year anniversaryJune 15, 2028
90-day window opensMarch 17, 2028
Last day to fileJune 15, 2028 (do not cut this close)
Set a hard calendar reminder for Month 18 (~December 2027) to begin I-829 preparation with your attorney. Also set an alert for the exact first day of the 90-day window.

The 2-year clock runs from your I-94 entry date — NOT the expiration date printed on your physical green card.

Use USCIS official calculator: uscis.gov/forms/when-to-file-your-petition-to-remove-conditions

Current I-829 Fee (April 2026)

$3,750 per petition — covers all derivatives (entire family files together). Payment via Form G-1450 (card) or G-1650 (ACH). Checks no longer accepted since October 28, 2025.

Fee history: USCIS raised I-829 to $9,525 in April 2024. A federal court ruled the increase unlawful in November 2025. Fee reverted to $3,750. A new proposed rule would set it at $7,860 — verify at uscis.gov/feecalculator at time of filing.
