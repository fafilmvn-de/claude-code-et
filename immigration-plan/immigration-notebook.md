## PART 1 — DAY 0: PORT OF ENTRY (POE)

### What You're Carrying

Each family member's passport contains an **Immigrant Visa (IV) foil** stamped by the US Embassy in Hanoi:
- Principal investor: **CR5** visa category
- Spouse and children: **CR6** visa category

The principal investor holds a **sealed NVC/Embassy packet** — **do not open this**. Hand it directly to the CBP officer.

### Step-by-Step POE Procedure

**Step 1 — CBP Primary Inspection**
- Proceed to the Primary Inspection counter
- Present: valid passport (with immigrant visa foil) + sealed embassy packet
- CBP officer scans passport, verifies biometrics, reviews visa
- Answer questions calmly and concisely (see table below)

**Step 2 — Secondary Inspection (expected for first-time immigrant entry)**
- First-time immigrant visa holders are routinely sent to secondary — this is normal
- CBP processes your sealed packet, formally admits you as a Conditional Permanent Resident
- Expect 30 min to 2+ hours depending on the airport and time of day

**Step 3 — I-551 ADIT Stamp**
- CBP stamps your passport with an I-551 stamp (also called ADIT stamp)
- Valid 6–12 months — functions as a temporary green card for employment (I-9), banking, SSN application, and driver's license during this period
- The physical green card (Form I-551) arrives by mail 45–90 days after entry

**Step 4 — After Customs**
- Collect luggage, clear Customs
- Your family is now officially Conditional Permanent Residents of the United States

### Documents to Carry at POE — Complete Checklist

**Essential (all family members):**
- [ ] Valid passport (valid 6+ months beyond entry date)
- [ ] Immigrant Visa foil in passport (stamped by Embassy Hanoi)
- [ ] Principal investor only: sealed NVC/Embassy packet — do NOT open
- [ ] I-526E or I-526 USCIS approval notices
- [ ] DS-260 immigrant visa application confirmation

**Supporting (have ready to produce):**
- [ ] I-797 approval notices for all USCIS filings
- [ ] Birth certificates — all family members (certified English translation)
- [ ] Marriage certificate (certified English translation)
- [ ] US address where green card will be mailed (written clearly on paper)
- [ ] EB-5 attorney contact information in the US
- [ ] Medical examination records from Embassy physician

**Optional but helpful:**
- [ ] Evidence of US bank account (if already established)
- [ ] Lease agreement or accommodation confirmation

### CBP Questions and How to Answer

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| "Purpose of entry?" | "I am entering as a conditional permanent resident under the EB-5 investor program." |
| "Where will you live?" | State your exact US address — have it written down. |
| "Do you intend to live permanently here?" | "Yes." |
| "What is your investment?" | "I invested in a USCIS-approved EB-5 Regional Center project." Keep it simple. |
| "Are you bringing currency over $10,000?" | Answer truthfully — amounts over $10,000 must be declared on FinCEN Form 105. |
| "Do you have a job?" | "Not yet. I am a conditional permanent resident searching for employment." |

> ⚠️ **Never lie to a CBP officer.** Misrepresentation is a federal crime and grounds for permanent inadmissibility.

### I-94 Check — Do This Within 24 Hours of Arrival

1. Go to **i94.cbp.dhs.gov**
2. Click "Get Most Recent I-94" — enter passport info
3. Verify all of the following:

| Field | What It Should Show |
|-------|---------------------|
| Class of Admission | **CR5** (investor) or **CR6** (spouse/children) |
| Admit Until Date | **"D/S"** (Duration of Status) — NOT a specific date |
| Entry Date | Must match actual arrival date — this starts your 2-year I-829 clock |
| Port of Entry | Must match the airport you landed at |

> ⚠️ If "Admit Until Date" shows a specific date (not D/S), contact your immigration attorney immediately.

**Print or screenshot the I-94 for every family member and save permanently.** This date determines your I-829 filing window.

> ⚠️ **CRITICAL (2025–2026 context):** The current administration has significantly increased CBP scrutiny of LPRs at ports of entry, including more secondary inspections and device searches. This is not unique to EB-5 holders. Remain calm, answer honestly, do not volunteer information beyond what is asked.

## PART 2 — ⚠️ URGENT: I-131 RE-ENTRY PERMITS (File Week 1–2)

> This is the highest-priority legal task after arrival if the spouse and/or children plan to return to Vietnam for the 2026–2027 school year.

### Why This Is Critical

If the spouse and children return to Vietnam for ~9–10 months (school year):
- Without a re-entry permit: near-certain green card abandonment risk for anyone absent 6+ months
- With a valid re-entry permit completed before departure: manageable risk

**The non-negotiable rule:** You **must be physically present in the US** both when you file I-131 AND when you attend the biometrics (fingerprint) appointment. You cannot do biometrics abroad.

### Who Needs I-131 in This Family

| Person | Situation | I-131 Needed? |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Husband (stays in US) | Primarily in US | Recommended if any trip >6 months |
| Wife (returning to Vietnam with kids) | ~9–10 months in Vietnam | **YES — CRITICAL** |
| Child 1 (returning to Vietnam) | ~9–10 months in Vietnam | **YES — CRITICAL** |
| Child 2 (returning to Vietnam) | ~9–10 months in Vietnam | **YES — CRITICAL** |

### I-131 Step-by-Step Process

**Form:** Form I-131 (Application for Travel Documents, Parole Documents, and Arrival/Departure Records) — download from uscis.gov/i-131. Each person files their own separate I-131.

**Step 1 — File as soon as possible after arrival (target: first week of July 2026)**
Documents per person:
- [ ] Completed Form I-131
- [ ] Copy of conditional green card — both sides
- [ ] Copy of passport biographical page
- [ ] Copy of I-94 record
- [ ] Two passport-style photos (per USCIS photo requirements)
- [ ] Form G-1450 (credit/debit card) or G-1650 (ACH bank payment)
- [ ] Request delivery to US Embassy Hanoi: specify in the application's embassy delivery section with Embassy's address

Mail to the USCIS Lockbox facility for your state of residence — check uscis.gov/i-131 for current address at time of filing.

**Step 2 — Biometrics (ASC) appointment**
- USCIS schedules biometrics 4–8 weeks after receipt
- Must attend in person at a designated Application Support Center (ASC) in the US
- Biometrics = fingerprints + photo + signature
- **Do not leave the US until biometrics are complete**

**Step 3 — Wait for permit (can depart after biometrics)**
- After biometrics are done, you may leave for Vietnam
- The permit is produced 1–4 months later and sent to the US Embassy in Hanoi
- Embassy notifies you when ready — pick up in person with passport + green card

### I-131 Current Fees (April 2026)
- **$630 per person** (paper filing, includes biometrics)
- Payment: Form G-1450 (credit/debit) or G-1650 (ACH) — checks/money orders no longer accepted since October 28, 2025
- Verify current fee at uscis.gov/g-1055 before filing

### Timeline for This Family

| Milestone | Target Date |
|-----------|-------------|
| Family arrives in US | June 2026 |
| File I-131 for wife + both children | **First week of July 2026** |
| Biometrics appointments (scheduled ~4–8 weeks after filing) | August 2026 |
| **Do not leave for Vietnam before biometrics are complete** | August/September 2026 |
| Re-entry permit sent to Hanoi Embassy | September–November 2026 |
| Children/wife depart for Vietnam school year | After biometrics — September 2026 |
| Return from Vietnam (before permit expires) | Summer 2027 — within 2 years of permit issuance |

### Re-Entry Permit Validity and Rules
- Valid for **up to 2 years** from issuance date
- Cannot be renewed — file a new I-131 for each permit needed (must be in US for biometrics)
- Does NOT guarantee re-entry — CBP can still question intent; but prevents green card from being treated as invalid for return
- Absence of 6–12 months WITH re-entry permit: CBP may question; prepare honest answers about school year necessity
- Absence of 12+ months WITH re-entry permit: manageable with strong US domicile evidence

### Abandonment Risk by Absence Duration

| Absence | Risk Level | Action Required |
|---------|------------|-----------------|
| Under 6 months | Low | No permit needed; routine entry |
| 6–12 months | Medium-High | **Re-entry permit required** |
| 12–24 months | High | Re-entry permit required + strong US ties documentation |
| Over 2 years | Near-certain abandonment | No re-entry permit valid beyond 2 years; SB-1 Returning Resident Visa (last resort, often denied) |

Apply for all 4 family members immediately. SSN is required for employment, taxes, banking, and credit.

**What you need:**
- [ ] Completed Form SS-5 (Application for a Social Security Card) — download from ssa.gov or pick up at the SSA office

**How to apply:**

### State Driver's License

**Why you need it quickly:**
- Required for most official purposes (banking, apartment rental, employment)
- Your international driver's license + IDP (from Vietnam) has a grace period — then you must have a US license

**Grace periods (from date of establishing state residency):**
| State | Grace Period |
|-------|-------------|
| Texas | 90 days |
| Washington | 30 days |
| North Carolina | 60 days |

> Get your **International Driving Permit (IDP)** from Vietnam's VRA (Vietnam Register Authority) BEFORE leaving Vietnam. IDP + Vietnamese license = right to drive in the US during grace period. Vietnam is NOT on any US state's reciprocity list, so you must take the written test AND road test.

**Process for each state:**
1. Visit the state DMV (TxDPS in TX, WA DOL in WA, NCDMV in NC)
2. Pass the knowledge test (written) — study the official state handbook (free online)
3. Pass the vision test
4. Pass the road test
5. Bring: passport, green card (or I-551 stamp), proof of US address, Social Security card (or confirmation you've applied)
6. Fee: typically $25–$50

**Get REAL ID-compliant driver's license** (required for domestic air travel from May 7, 2025 onward):
- Bring one document for identity (passport or green card)
- One document for SSN (Social Security card or W-2)
- Two documents for state residency (lease, utility bill, bank statement)

### ACA Health Insurance

**You are eligible:** EB-5 conditional green card holders are fully eligible for ACA Marketplace plans.

**Special Enrollment Period (SEP):** You have **60 days** from the date you become a lawful permanent resident to enroll without waiting for the Open Enrollment Period. This SEP starts on your date of entry at POE.

**How to enroll:**
1. Go to healthcare.gov
2. Create an account
3. Enter household information (family of 4, projected income for the year)
4. Browse plans — compare bronze/silver/gold tiers
5. Select a plan and pay the first premium

**Important rules:**
- **5-year Medicaid bar:** As new LPRs, you are barred from federally-funded Medicaid for 5 years from your admission date. Do NOT apply for Medicaid — you do not qualify yet. Some states have state-funded programs; check your state.
- For ACA Marketplace plans, you ARE eligible immediately

**US resume format — non-negotiable rules:**
- 1–2 pages maximum (senior = 2 pages acceptable)

## PART 7 — MONTH 2–12: ONGOING OPERATIONS

### US Taxes — Year 1 Critical Guidance

**Dual-status year (2026):**
Your first US tax year is complex:
- January 1 – May 31, 2026 (approx): Non-Resident Alien (NRA) for tax purposes
- June 1 – December 31, 2026 (approx): Resident Alien for tax purposes

You file a **dual-status tax return** for 2026. This is complex — **hire a US-Vietnam CPA for Year 1.** Do not attempt this yourself.

**From Day 1 of US residency:** All worldwide income is reportable to the IRS. This includes:
- Vietnamese employment income (if any)
- Rental income from Vietnamese property
- Interest/dividends from Vietnamese bank accounts
- Capital gains from any Vietnamese investments

**FBAR — FinCEN Form 114 (Annual Bank Reporting):**
- Required if the combined maximum balance in all foreign (non-US) financial accounts exceeds **$10,000 at any point during the calendar year**
- File at bsaefiling.fincen.treas.gov — NOT with your tax return
- Deadline: April 15 (automatic extension to October 15 — file by Oct 15)
- **Non-willful penalty:** Up to ~$15,000 per violation per year
- **Willful failure penalty:** Up to $100,000 or 50% of account balance per year (criminal charges possible)
- Report ALL Vietnamese bank accounts (savings, checking, business) if combined max balance exceeded $10K

**FATCA — Form 8938 (Foreign Financial Assets):**
- Filed WITH your Form 1040 (tax return)
- Thresholds for Married Filing Jointly living in US: Report if total foreign financial assets exceed $100,000 on last day of year OR $150,000 on any day during year
- Report: Vietnamese bank accounts, Vietnamese investment accounts, Vietnamese business interests

**No US-Vietnam Tax Treaty:** Vietnam and the US do not have a tax treaty. You cannot claim treaty exemptions. You can only claim the **Foreign Tax Credit (Form 1116)** to avoid double taxation — Vietnamese tax paid can be credited against US tax owed on the same income.

**Key dates:**
| Deadline | Action |
|----------|--------|
| April 15, 2027 | File 2026 US federal income tax return (or extension request Form 4868) |
| April 15, 2027 | FBAR for 2026 (auto-extended to October 15) |
| June 15, Sept 15, Jan 15, April 15 | Quarterly estimated tax payments (if self-employed or significant non-wage income) |

**Find a US-Vietnam CPA:** Search for CPAs specializing in "expatriate" or "international tax" or "dual-status year" returns. Expect $500–$2,000 for Year 1 dual-status return. Vietnamese-speaking CPAs exist in all three target cities.

## PART 8 — I-829: REMOVING CONDITIONS ON PERMANENT RESIDENCE

> This is the second most critical legal deadline after the I-131 re-entry permit.

### Calculating Your 90-Day Filing Window

The I-829 must be filed within the **90-day period immediately preceding the 2nd anniversary of your date of admission**.

**Your anniversary date = your exact POE entry date + 2 years**

Example (if you enter June 15, 2026):
| Milestone | Date |
|-----------|------|
| Entry date | June 15, 2026 |
| 2-year anniversary | June 15, 2028 |
| **90-day window opens** | **March 17, 2028** |
| **Last day to file** | **June 15, 2028** |

> ⚠️ **Set a hard calendar reminder for Month 18 (approximately December 2027) to begin I-829 preparation with your attorney.** Also set an alert for the exact first day of the 90-day window.

**Use the USCIS official calculator:** uscis.gov/forms/when-to-file-your-petition-to-remove-conditions

**The 2-year clock runs from your I-94 entry date — NOT the expiration date printed on your physical green card.** Check your I-94 record to confirm the exact date.

### Current I-829 Filing Fee (April 2026)

**Current fee: $3,750 per petition** (covers all derivatives on the same petition — entire family files together)

> Fee history: USCIS raised the I-829 fee to $9,525 in April 2024. In November 2025, a federal court (*Moody et al. v. Mayorkas*, District of Colorado) ruled the increase unlawful because USCIS failed to complete the required fee study. The fee reverted to $3,750. A new proposed rule (October 2025) would set I-829 at $7,860 — check uscis.gov/feecalculator at time of filing.

**Payment:** Form G-1450 (credit/debit) or G-1650 (ACH). Checks/money orders not accepted since October 28, 2025.

### I-829 Evidence Package

Work with your EB-5 attorney and regional center to compile:

| Category | Documents |
|----------|-----------|
| Identity | Copies of passports + both sides of green cards |
| Investment made | Wire transfer records, escrow release docs, bank statements |
| Investment at risk | Subscription agreement, operating agreement, offering memorandum |
| Job creation | For regional center: economist's report showing 10+ jobs created/preserved via econometric model; payroll/W-2 records if direct jobs |
| Project status | Construction progress, certificate of occupancy, financial statements of NCE/JCE |
| Sustainment (pre-RIA I-526) | Evidence capital remained invested throughout 2-year conditional period |
| Sustainment (post-RIA I-526E) | Evidence capital invested for minimum 2 years from deployment date |
| Family documents | Birth certificates + translations, marriage certificate + translation, I-94 records for each |

**Pre-RIA vs. Post-RIA sustainment — confirm with your attorney which applies:**
| Filing Date | Requirement |
|-------------|-------------|
| I-526 filed before March 15, 2022 (pre-RIA) | Investment must be sustained throughout the entire 2-year conditional residency |
| I-526/I-526E filed on or after March 15, 2022 (post-RIA) | Investment must remain invested for 2 years from deployment date — no requirement to sustain through all of conditional residency |

### Current I-829 Processing Times (April 2026)

- USCIS official published estimate: ~30 months (80th percentile)
- Actual median (IIUSA FY2025 Q2 data): **8.2 months**
- Approval rate: **94%** in FY2025

**After filing I-829:**
- USCIS issues a receipt notice (Form I-797) — keep this permanently
- Your conditional status is automatically extended until USCIS decides
- Carry the I-797 receipt + your expired green card together for proof of status during adjudication

### Consequences of Late I-829 Filing

- Status automatically terminates as of the 2-year anniversary
- Investor becomes immediately removable (deportable)
- USCIS may issue a Notice to Appear (NTA) initiating removal proceedings
- No grace period — do not miss this deadline
